## 1. Title

**"Study Why You Should Never Own Property in Your Own Name"**

---

## 2. Buy Property Through a Limited Company to Avoid Section 24 Tax

- Section 24 restricts tax relief on mortgage interest for personally owned rental properties.
- Holding property in a Limited Company allows you to deduct 100% of mortgage interest, reducing taxable profits significantly.

---

## 3. Use Trusts to Own Assets While Paying Zero Inheritance Tax

- If you own assets personally, your heirs could pay 40%+ inheritance tax.
- Placing assets in a trust removes them from your personal estate, passing wealth to heirs tax-free while you still control everything.

---

## 4. Set Up a Holding Company for Ultimate Tax Efficiency

- Instead of holding assets personally, structure them under a holding company, which owns multiple businesses or properties.
- Profits can be reinvested tax-free within the company instead of being withdrawn and taxed as personal income.

---

## 5. Show Business Assets as Collateral for Bigger Loans

- Banks prefer lending to companies with business assets rather than personal borrowers.
- By moving high-value assets into a corporate entity, you qualify for larger loans, lower interest rates, and better financing options.

---

## 6. Use Offshore Companies for 0% Corporate Tax

- Certain jurisdictions (like Dubai, Cayman Islands, or Singapore) have 0% corporate tax.
- The wealthy set up companies there to legally shelter income from high-tax countries, reinvesting more profits for rapid growth.

---

## 7. Create a "Double Company" Structure to Reduce Taxes Further

- One company earns revenue while the second company charges management fees, reducing taxable profits in the main company.
- Used by elite entrepreneurs to legally shift profits into low-tax business accounts.

---

## 8. Lease, Don't Buy, Expensive Assets for Maximum Tax Deductions

- Buying cars, offices, or equipment personally means no tax benefit.
- Leasing under a business means 100% of payments are tax-deductible, effectively making them free after tax savings.

---

## 9. Use Corporate Credit Instead of Personal Credit

- The wealthy never take loans in their name; they use business credit.
- Business loans don't show on personal credit reports, keeping debt-to-income ratios low while securing massive funding.

---

## 10. Move Money Between Multiple Entities to Reduce Tax Exposure

- High-tax countries take huge percentages of direct income, but moving funds between multiple businesses in different tax jurisdictions can legally minimize tax liability.
- Smart investors strategically shift income before withdrawing it.

---

## 11. Take Income as Dividends, Not Salary (Pays Less Tax)

- Salaries are taxed heavily.
- The wealthy take income as dividends, taxed at much lower rates (as low as 7.5% instead of 40%+ on salary).

---

## 12. Buy Property in a Tax-Free REIT (Real Estate Investment Trust)

- Personal property sales trigger capital gains tax.
- Investing through a REIT allows tax-free gains and higher lending power, since banks favor institutional investors over individuals.

---

## 13. Use Life Insurance as a Wealth Transfer Vehicle

- Standard inheritance can be taxed at 40%.
- Billionaires structure their estate with life insurance trusts, passing money to heirs completely tax-free.

---

## 14. Hide Business Profits by Holding Intellectual Property (IP)

- Instead of taking direct income, businesses charge royalty fees to an offshore entity that owns their trademarks, patents, or branding.
- This moves profits to low-tax countries while keeping revenue streams legal.

---

## 15. Use a Nominee Director for Privacy & Asset Protection

- Owning assets personally makes you an easy target for lawsuits.
- The rich use nominee directors to hide ownership of companies, protecting their name from public records and legal attacks.
